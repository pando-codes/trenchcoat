# Product Vision

## The Future We're Creating

Every engineer using AI agents knows exactly which ones are working — and which ones aren't. Teams make confident decisions about where to invest in AI tooling, and budget owners can see the value of every dollar spent on AI. The fog between "we're using AI" and "our AI is delivering ROI" no longer exists.

## Why This Matters

AI agents are proliferating faster than anyone's ability to evaluate them. Engineers are running dozens of sessions a week with Claude — using skills, tools, subagents, and hooks — but have no clear picture of what's actually valuable. Budget decision makers are approving AI spend based on gut feel. Without visibility, teams either over-invest in agents that underperform or under-invest in ones that are quietly delivering massive leverage.

The problem isn't a lack of AI capability. It's a lack of clarity about the AI already in use.

## Who We Serve

**Primary: The Individual Engineer**

An engineer at a software company who uses Claude Code daily. They're curious, data-driven, and slightly anxious about whether their AI usage is productive or just expensive noise. They want a fast, honest answer to: "Is this actually working?"

**Secondary: The Technical Lead / Manager Building Upward Visibility**

Someone who needs to report AI tool ROI to engineering leadership or a budget owner. They don't need raw telemetry — they need a clean summary that answers "what are we spending, and what are we getting for it?" They are often the same person as the primary user, wearing a different hat.

## The World Today vs. Tomorrow

**Today:** An engineer finishes a Claude Code session and has no idea what it cost, which tools fired, or whether the subagents they invoked did anything useful. A manager preparing a quarterly AI budget review pieces together rough usage estimates from billing dashboards that don't map to outcomes.

**Tomorrow:** After every session, an engineer can see exactly what ran, what it cost, and which components drove the most activity. A manager opens a single dashboard, selects a date range, and has a board-ready summary of team AI usage and cost attribution — in under a minute.

## Guiding Beliefs

- **Clarity beats completeness.** An engineer who understands 3 key metrics acts better than one drowning in 30.
- **Value is revealed through usage patterns, not raw events.** The question isn't "what happened" — it's "what mattered."
- **Cost without context is noise.** Spend data is only useful when paired with activity data that explains what it bought.
- **The individual engineer is the power user.** If it doesn't serve them first, it won't serve anyone.
