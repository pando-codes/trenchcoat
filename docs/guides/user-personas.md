# User Personas

## Primary: The Individual Engineer

**Who they are:** A software engineer at a startup or tech company using Claude Code daily as a core part of their workflow. They're early-adopting, pragmatic, and curious about whether their AI usage is productive.

**What they're doing:** Running Claude Code sessions to write code, debug, research, and automate tasks. Using skills, tools, and subagents without a clear sense of how each is performing.

**What they want to know:**
- Which tools and agents did I actually use today?
- What did this session cost me?
- Which agents are pulling their weight vs. which are just running up tokens?

**Desired outcome after using Trenchcoat:**
> "I can see that my refactoring skill runs twice a day but accounts for 40% of my token spend. Now I know where to focus."

**What they don't want:** Dashboards that require setup, dashboards full of raw events they have to interpret themselves, or dashboards that feel like work to use.

**How they discover Trenchcoat:** Plugin marketplace, word-of-mouth from other Claude Code power users.

**Success signal:** They check Trenchcoat at the end of a session or at the start of the next day. They form opinions about their agent usage based on data they got from Trenchcoat.

---

## Secondary: The Technical Lead / Engineering Manager

**Who they are:** A senior engineer or engineering manager who sponsors AI tooling adoption on their team. They use Claude Code themselves but also need to communicate about team-wide usage to leadership.

**What they're doing:** Periodically reviewing team AI tool spend and trying to make the case (or the cut) for specific tools and platforms. Often preparing a monthly or quarterly summary for a VP of Engineering or a budget owner who is not technical.

**What they want to know:**
- How much is our team spending on Claude Code this month?
- Which team members are the heaviest users, and what are they using it for?
- Is our AI spend going up or down, and why?

**Desired outcome after using Trenchcoat:**
> "I pulled up Trenchcoat, selected the last 30 days, and had everything I needed for the budget review in under 5 minutes."

**What they don't want:** A tool that requires them to run queries, export raw data, or train non-technical stakeholders to use.

**How they discover Trenchcoat:** An engineer on their team is already using it and surfaces the team-level view to them.

**Success signal:** They create a team workspace, check in once a week or once a month, and share the dashboard (or an export) with stakeholders without additional data wrangling.

---

## Non-User (Explicitly Excluded for Now)

**The Enterprise IT / Security Buyer:** Interested in compliance, data residency, and SSO. Trenchcoat does not yet solve for this buyer. Do not build to their requirements until core personas are deeply served.

**The AI Agent Developer (product builder):** Building an AI-powered product and wanting to meter and bill their end users. Adjacent to Trenchcoat's value but a distinct product motion. Not the current focus.
